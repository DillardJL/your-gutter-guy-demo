Signs Your Gutters Need Repair

Gutters don't usually fail all at once — they give you warning signs before a small problem becomes an expensive one. Knowing what to look for can save you from foundation repairs, rotted fascia, or flooded basements. Here's what to watch for:

  • ✓
    Water dripping behind gutters. If water is running between the gutter and the fascia board rather than through the downspout, the back of the gutter may have separated or the slope has shifted. This leads directly to fascia rot if left untreated.
  • ✓
    Sagging or pulling away from the fascia. Gutters that visibly droop or pull away from the house have typically lost their hangers — the hidden brackets that hold them in place. This is often caused by the weight of standing water, heavy debris, or ice buildup over winter.
  • ✓
    Visible cracks or holes. Even small cracks in the gutter body allow water to escape. In aluminum gutters, cracks are most common at stress points — corners, end caps, and areas that have taken impact from falling limbs or ladders.
  • ✓
    Water marks on siding. Streaks, staining, or mildew on the siding directly below your gutters are a strong sign that water is overflowing or leaking at a specific point along the run. The stain marks the location of the problem.
  • ✓
    Overflowing during rain. Gutters that overflow during normal rain events are either clogged, pitched incorrectly, undersized for your roof's drainage area, or pulling away from the roofline. All of these are repairable.
  • ✓
    Rust or dark staining on the gutter. While aluminum gutters don't rust, standing water and organic debris can cause dark staining and surface pitting that weakens the gutter over time. Steel gutters can develop true rust that eventually eats through the metal.
  • ✓
    Peeling paint near gutters. Paint that bubbles or peels directly beneath a gutter run is a classic sign of chronic moisture exposure. Even if the gutter looks intact from below, it may be leaking at the back seam or at a joint that's hidden from view.

What We Repair

Your Gutter Guy LLC handles the full range of gutter repair work — from a single leaking end cap to storm-damaged sections that need to be completely re-hung. Here's what Jeff repairs on a regular basis:

Leaks & Holes

Small holes and pinhole leaks in the gutter body are sealed using gutter sealant or patched with flashing material, depending on the size and location of the damage. Corroded or severely compromised sections may be replaced.

Sagging & Loose Gutters

Gutters that have sagged or pulled away from the fascia are re-hung using new hidden hanger brackets, re-pitched for proper drainage slope, and re-secured to solid wood. If the fascia itself has rotted, that needs to be addressed first — see our fascia repair service.

Separated Joints & Seams

Sectional gutters with open joints are re-sealed with high-quality gutter caulk or fitted with new slip-joint connectors. When joints repeatedly fail in the same location, it may indicate a pitch or support issue that needs to be corrected at the same time.

Clogged or Damaged Downspouts

Downspouts that are clogged, crushed, or disconnected from the gutter are flushed, cleared, straightened, or replaced as needed. We also reposition downspout extensions that are directing water too close to the foundation.

Gutters Pulling Away from the House

When gutters detach from the roofline edge, water gets behind the system and directly onto the fascia and soffit. Jeff re-attaches and re-pitches the gutter run, inspecting the surrounding wood for damage before completing the repair.

Storm & Impact Damage

Fallen branches, heavy ice loads, and high winds are common in the Roanoke Valley, especially in spring and during fall storms. Jeff can assess storm-damaged sections quickly and handle emergency repairs to prevent water intrusion while you get back to normal.

Repair vs. Replace: What Makes Sense?

Not every gutter problem means you need a full replacement. Conversely, not every "quick fix" is worth throwing money at. Here's how Jeff thinks about the repair vs. replace decision — and what he'll tell you honestly when he comes out for an estimate.

When Repair Makes Sense

  • → Damage is localized to one section or one joint while the rest of the system is structurally sound
  • → Gutters are relatively young (under 10–12 years) and otherwise in good condition
  • → The issue is mechanical — a hanger that pulled out, a downspout that disconnected, an end cap that's come loose
  • → Storm impact caused damage to an otherwise solid system
  • → Budget constraints make a targeted repair a better near-term choice

When Replacement Is the Better Call

  • → Gutters are 15 years or older and showing widespread rust, corrosion, or multiple leaks
  • → Multiple sections are sagging or pulling away at the same time — a sign of systemic hanger failure
  • → You're dealing with several separate problems at once, and the cumulative repair cost approaches or exceeds replacement cost
  • → The fascia is so deteriorated that new gutters need to be installed regardless
  • → You want to upgrade to seamless gutters or add guards at the same time

Jeff will always give you his honest read. If a repair will do the job well and last, he'll say so. If replacement is the smarter investment, he'll explain why — without any pressure. Call or request a free estimate and he'll take a look.

Common Questions About Gutter Repair

How much does gutter repair cost?⌄

Gutter repair costs vary widely depending on the type and extent of the damage. Resealing a joint or patching a small hole is significantly less expensive than re-hanging a full gutter run or replacing damaged sections. Jeff provides free on-site estimates so you know exactly what you're looking at before any work begins — call (540) 354-1662 or use the estimate request form on this page.

Can I repair gutters myself?⌄

Minor repairs — like applying fresh sealant to a small joint gap on a single-story home — are within reach for a handy homeowner. However, sagging gutters require re-pitching and new hangers, which means working on a ladder while holding hardware and measuring slope. Leaks that originate behind the gutter or at the fascia often involve hidden damage that's hard to assess without experience. For anything structural, leak-related, or on a two-story home, it's worth having a pro handle it safely and correctly.

Turnaround time depends on the current schedule, but most repair jobs can be scheduled within a few days of your call.
